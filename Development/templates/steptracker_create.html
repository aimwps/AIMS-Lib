Types of tracker

Here's the types of trackers and example use cases.

'Count Up': You're building your meditation behaviour. You want to do atleast 20 minutes meditation a day. But, you know 1 minute is a realistic starting point. Start progressing towards that 20 minutes with a count up tracker.

'Count Down': You've decided to quit smoking and want to smoke 0 cigarettes a day. Currently you smoke 20, start counting down to success with this tracker.

'Yes or No': Having decided to be more organised. You are going to make your bed every morning. Did you complete it, yes or no? (This option will disable a lot of further questions).

The action you're tracking

Here's how to enter an action

'For counting trackers': It's simply a verb, if you're trying to run more, 'run' is the keyword. Want to do more yoga? 'practise yoga' would be perfect.

'Yes or No trackers': You'll need to enter a statement. "I made my bed" or "I got out of bed wthout an alarm clock" are some examples.

Unit of measurement

Counting trackers only: Enter the unit you're recording. For example. An action of run might track 'miles' or 'minutes'. Or, an action of read could be 'pages of a book' or 'new words'.

Minimum Expectation

Counting trackers only: Enter the minimum you expect of yourself, this should be close to what you currently achieve. If you're minimizing screen time and are currently at 8 hours a day you need to be realistic. Cutting down to 7 is progress and worthy minimum expectation of yourself. So, enter 7.

Awesome performance

Counting trackers only: This is what you have envisioned for yourself. If you're counting down to 0 of something in your life. The 0 is your awesome performance. It is value you you're stepping towards that truly builds the behaviour.

Minimum show allowed

For all trackers:Some days the world kicks you in the butt. Never give up. If you check this you'll allow yourself a minimum show up statement.

Minimum show statement: You've been out running miles every morning before work. You're proud, you're getting somewhere, then, you don't sleep that night. There's nothing left in the tank and you dread coming off the pedal for the fear you'll stop completely. A minimum show statement would be "I put running clothes on and walked to the end of my road".

Record Frequency

Record frequency: how often you will record the action

Record start date

Record start date: defaults to today. Those organised can plan in future tracking starting points by adding a custom start date.

Record multiple per period

Record start date: Only applies to counting trackers, if you want to record everytime you take an action check this box. For example if you're aiming to read 40 minutes a day you can submit 8 x 5 minute blocks to reach your aim.

Complete Criteria

Complete criteria:How you know you've made real progression.

Complete Value

Complete value :The quantity of times that you complete you're action either conseuctively or in total
